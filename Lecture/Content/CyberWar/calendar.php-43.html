Cyber Reconnaissance


Learning Outcomes

After completing these activities you should be able to:

  • Perform active reconnaissance of a target host using Network Mapper (Nmap) / command line tool.
  • Perform active reconnaissance of a target host using Open Vulnerability Assessment System (Sparta) / GUI tool.
  • Perform analysis of the vulnerabilities discovered and pair them with an exploit.

Instructions

You will have three tasks to complete for this lab, which correlate to completing the two worksheets below. One for each of the active reconnaissance target scans described in the learning outcomes, and linked below. As an aspiring offensive cyber operator, it is important that you review and understand the Commander's intent. Commander's intent succinctly describes what constitutes success for the operation. It includes the operation's purpose and the conditions that define the end state. In other words, it is the requirement, and makes explicit the objectives for successfully completing your assignment (mission).

Scenario / Commanders Intent

Our Cyberspace Forces are trained, equipped and ready to respond to any aggression against the United States of America, or our NATO allies. The (fictional) country of Zendia has become aggressive and hostile towards our ability to freely maneuver the Straits of Hormuz. The Straits of Hormuz is a strait between the Persian Gulf and the Gulf of Oman. It provides the only sea passage from the Persian Gulf to the open ocean and is one of the world's most strategically important choke points. About 20% of the world's petroleum (about 35% of the petroleum traded by sea) passes through the strait, making it a highly important strategic location for international trade.

Students will leverage their training and tools provided to successfully recon, survey, analyze vulnerabilities of the adversary systems. Specifically, you need to recon, survey, and analyze vulnerabilities of the onboard navigation system for ships used by Zendia’s Navy. The ships include a submarine, destroyer, amphibious assault, and patrol ship. Each of these four ships have different operating systems and services running onboard. Our N2 (Intelligence) has provided us with the following information about each system:

Type of ShipOSCyber Recon Tool
SubmarineLinuxNetwork Mapper (Nmap)
DestroyerWindowsNetwork Mapper (Nmap)
Amphibious AssaultLinuxSparta (Note: same VM as Submarine Fall AY2019)
PatrolWindowsSparta (Note: same VM as Destroyer Fall AY2019)

Students will use multiple cyber reconnaissance tools to achieve the objective. Our goal is to obfuscate our actions by using multiple tools. Each tool is different, but ultimately provides similar functionality and results. Given that, each tool may or may not have a signature the adversary could use to attribute our actions.

Using the tools provided, and the worksheet instructions below, execute the active reconnaissance operation against each target (type of ship). Document your findings via the worksheets below to begin.
