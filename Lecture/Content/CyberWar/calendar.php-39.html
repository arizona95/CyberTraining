Lab 6: LAN-WLAN

During this lab exercise, students will interact with two networking demos to help reinforce how information is transported across networks. The lab instructions include explicit instructions as to what the students must do. As the students work through the lab, they will need to answer a few questions. When they are done, they will submit the completed worksheet for credit.

Learning Outcomes

After completing these activities you should be able to:

  • Describe the relationship between the TCP/IP Stack and the construction of a Local Network - devices used, Host, Base Station.
  • Describe the relationship between the TCP/IP Stack and the construction of a network of Local Networks - devices used, Host, Base Station, Router.
  • Appropriately use these commands and tools and explain their output: ipconfig, netstat, arp, ping, tracert, netcat. Interpret their output to deduce information about network hosts, topology and services, and to construct a physical wired network and an encrypted wireless network, both connected to a simple internet.

A. Notes

  • If you have trouble with the graphics of either demo, you may need to zoom in or zoom out, depending on your screen resolution. Use Ctrl + Mousewheel or Ctrl and +/- to zoom in or out.

  • If you ever got lost, you can always reset the associated demo and start from the beginning of the section.

B. Introduction

  1. Open this link in a new tab: https://www.usna.edu/CyberDept/sy110/calendar.php?type=resources&event=17. Click “Network/Inter-network Demo.”

  2. This demo shows two local networks in detail connected via the Internet (other routers and perhaps other local networks). Answer Question 1 on the Blackboard assignment.

  3. Let’s make sure we understand how these devices would logically be connected (Hint: https://www.usna.edu/CyberDept/sy110/calendar.php?key=4d9fac195391c55f945717e89d349634f6a9d0db&type=class&event=24). Answer Questions 2-3 on the Blackboard assignment. These devices can communicate directly with one another. Answer Question 4 on the Blackboard assignment.

  4. The information inside each box tells us the network configuration settings for that machine. Answer Questions 5-7 on the Blackboard assignment.

C. Moving Data Inside of a Local Network

  1. First we are going to transmit information between two hosts inside of a local network. Click “Add Message.” Enter a short message of your choice to transmit across the network and click “Ok.” Click on Host 1. Host 1 should now be outlined in green. Click on Host 2. Host 2 should now be outlined in red. The messages on the right reflect the settings for sender and receiver that are establishing.

  2. Host 1 needs to determine whether the desired receiver is inside the same local network or not. Click “Next Step” three times. The messages on the right will update to show high-level how Host 1 is identifying Host 2’s relative network location. Host 1 uses both of their IP address and its subnet mask to perform a calculation that provides the answer. Answer Question 8 on the Blackboard assignment.

  3. Click “Next Step.” Host 1 is now looking up Host 2’s MAC address in its ARP table. Answer Questions 9-10 on the Blackboard assignment.

  4. Now that Host 1 knows Host 2’s MAC address, it is ready to send the data. Click “Next Step.” Answer Question 11 on the Blackboard assignment.

  5. Click “Next Step.” The data is now forwarded to Host 2. Answer Question 12 on the Blackboard assignment.

  6. Click “Next Step.” Read the message on the right side of the screen. Click “Reset Demo.”

D. Moving Data Between Different Local Networks

  1. Now we are going to transmit information between two hosts that are part of two different local networks. Click “Add Message.” Enter a short message of your choice to transmit across the network and click “Ok.” Click on Host 1. Host 1 should now be outlined in green. Click on Host 3. Host 3 should now be outlined in red. The messages on the right reflect the settings for sender and receiver that are establishing.

  2. Host 1 needs to determine whether the desired receiver is inside the same local network or not. Click “Next Step” three times. The messages on the right will update to show high-level how Host 1 is identifying Host 3’s relative network location. Host 1 uses both of their IP address and its subnet mask to perform a calculation that provides the answer. Answer Question 13 on the Blackboard assignment.

  3. Answer Question 14 on the Blackboard assignment.

  4. Click “Next Step” twice. Answer Questions 15-16 on the Blackboard assignment.

  5. Click “Next Step” twice. The information has been routed/forwarded to Router 2. Answer Question 17 on the Blackboard assignment.

  6. Click “Next Step” four times. Observe the data being forwarded to Host 3. Answer Question 18 on the Blackboard assignment.

E. Network Address Translation (NAT)

  1. Go to: https://www.usna.edu/CyberDept/sy110/calendar.php?type=resources&event=17.

  2. Click “Network Address Translation (NAT) Demo.”

  3. This demo shows a host (your laptop) and the USNA Gateway on the USNA local network on the left and a web server on the right, connected to one another via the Internet. It will simulate sending traffic from “Your Laptop” to a web server and receiving the response.

  4. Answer Questions 19-20 on the Blackboard assignment. (Hint: https://www.usna.edu/CyberDept/sy110/calendar.php?type=class&event=20)

  5. Answer Questions 21-22 on the Blackboard assignment.

  6. Click “Begin Demo.” Answers Questions 23-29 on the Blackboard assignment.

  7. Click “Next Step.” Wait for the USNA Gateway to turn blue. This demo does not show the data being forwarded to the Gateway by a switch, but the process is similar to that from Part C. Click “Next Step.” The new box that pops up between the USNA Gateway and the Internet is what the USNA Gateway transmits on behalf of “Your Laptop.” Answer Questions 30-34 on the Blackboard assignment.

  8. Click “Next Step.” A new entry is added to the USNA Gateway’s NAT table. Visually identify where the data in the NAT table entry came from.

  9. Click “Next Step.” Wait for the “foobar.com” web server to turn blue. Answer Question 35 on the Blackboard assignment.

  10. Click “Next Step” twice. The “foobar.com” web server is transmitting a response. Answer Question 36 on the Blackboard assignment.

  11. Click “Next Step” and wait for the USNA Gateway to turn blue. Click “Next Step.” Answer Question 37 on the Blackboard assignment.

  12. Answer Question 38 on the Blackboard assignment. (Hint: https://www.usna.edu/CyberDept/sy110/calendar.php?type=class&event=21)

  13. Click “Next Step.” The demo is complete.
